Two-day ECS Event at 2024 A-RIP Planning Workshop

July 20 – 21, 2024

The APARC Reanalysis Intercomparison Project (A-RIP) is sponsoring a 2-day Early-Career Scientist (ECS) event from July 20 to 21 at the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics (LASP), Space Science Center (SPSC). The A-RIP ECS event is scheduled to follow the 2024 Quadrennial Ozone Symposium (QOS) in Boulder, Colorado, USA (July 15 to 19), which will attract climate researchers from around the world.

We are targeting Early-Career Scientists (i.e. BSc, MSc and PhD students, as well as postdoctoral fellows) from all over the world who plan to attend the QOS and A-RIP meetings in Boulder, along with local students and researchers. Participation may be limited (we anticipate approximately 10 places). ECS applications from the Global South are particularly encouraged. The ECS event is organized by Mohamadou Diallo (ECS and outreach coordinator), Sean Davis (local organizer), V. Lynn Harvey (local organizer), Gloria Manney (A-RIP co-lead), Jonathon Wright (A-RIP co-lead), and Masatomo Fujiwara (A-RIP co-lead).

Logistical details

Participation in the ECS event is free of charge. Coffee breaks, lunches and accommodation are also included for the first day (July 20). Financial assistance is available for those who need to cover the cost of flight changes for at least a limited number of ECSs.

The deadline for applications is July 8, 2024. For applications, please send an email to A-RIP (aripworkshop2024@gmail.com) with the subject “ECS Event Application” and include information on your background, university, and country of origin, as well as how you think this event will help you in your current or future projects.

The A-RIP Workshop

The A-RIP workshop will be held from July 22 to 24, 2024, at the SPSC. For more information please see the preliminary programme.

Schedule

The ECS event will prioritize informal, small group, and one-on-one activities to bring ECS together with both other ECS from diverse regions and backgrounds and senior scientists.
